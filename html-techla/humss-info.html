HUMSS strand

What is the HUMSS strand?

The HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences) strand is an educational program or track offered in some high schools that focuses on subjects related to the social sciences, humanities, and arts. It aims to provide students with a broad understanding of human behavior, society, culture, and creative expression.

Strand Overview

The HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences) strand offers a diverse range of subjects, including humanities, social sciences, research skills, arts, communication and media studies. Students explore literature, philosophy, history, and languages, gaining insights into human expression and historical events. They also study psychology, sociology, anthropology, and political science to understand human behavior and social structures. The strand emphasizes research skills, critical thinking, and effective communication. Students have opportunities to engage with the arts, develop creative abilities, and appreciate various art forms. The HUMSS strand promotes community engagement and social awareness through community service activities and involvement with local organizations.
