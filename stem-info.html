STEM Strand

What is the STEM strand?

The STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) strand is an educational program or track offered in some high schools that provide students with a strong foundation in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics subjects. This program aims to prepare students for future careers that require a strong background in these subjects. The STEM strand aims to cultivate a new generation of professionals who are equipped to tackle complex challenges and contribute to scientific advancements and technological innovation.

Strand Overview

Students study biology, chemistry, physics, and environmental science in the science component. The technology component covers computer science, information technology, and programming. Engineering introduces design principles and various disciplines like civil, mechanical, electrical, and aerospace engineering. Mathematics includes algebra, calculus, statistics, and geometry. The strand emphasizes integrating these disciplines to solve complex problems and encourages practical applications through robotics competitions, engineering projects, and internships. It fosters critical thinking, innovation, and collaboration.
